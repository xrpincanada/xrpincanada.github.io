How to purchase XRP in Canadian dollars on a Canadian Exchange a step by step guide

I wanted to write an article after finding it to be a bit of pain getting straight forward details on how to purchase XRP in Canada. I was not looking to use several different exchanges outside of Canada, changing Canadian dollars to a different fiat currency, or purchasing bitcoin so that I could then buy XRP. Those methods are confusing, convoluted, costly, and can leave you holding XRP without a way to get your money back into your account.

What I was looking for in an exchange was straight forward:

  • A reputable Canadian owned and operated crypto exchange.
  • An exchange that accepted Canadian dollars with a few different methods of funding.
  • An exchange that had best practices such as cold storage, SSL and 2factor authentication (2fa).
  • An exchange that would allow withdrawals in Canadian dollars back to my bank account.
  • An exchange that had not had any security breaches with either cryptocurrency or personal data.
  • One that was easy to use!

The best cyrpto exchange I had come across was Coinsquare. At the time it seemed to have the most history in Canada as a crypto exchange and from what I could find no data breach, boasting a proprietary security system and not one digital coin lost!

These are the steps that I took to get some XRP.

Step 1: Starting an account with basic info and KYC (Know Your Customer) details.

As a customer, they require that you submit an address, a valid piece of government ID with a photo, a proof of address document that includes your full address and an unobstructed picture of yourself (selfie). They also require verification via a mobile device. The process was straight forward and took only a day or so at the time to become verified. The upper right-hand corner of the account page shows your verification status, next to your username in a blue box with white text.

Step 2: Turning on 2 Factor Authentication

Before you add funds to your account, it's always a good idea to turn on 2-factor authentication. This method of authentication usually involves an app or hardware device that is paired with your account, adding a layer of security. Coinsqaure uses the Google 2FA app that's available on the Google Play store for Android or App Store for Apple users.

You can turn on this service by navigating to your account name in the top right-hand corner. Click on your account name and then settings, on the left-hand side click 2-Factor Authentication and select Google Authenticator. Walk through the process of scanning the QR code from your mobile device and verifying the setup (Do not take a picture of the QR code or print it out as someone else could pair an additional device to your account). When you believe you have set it up, log out of your account and sign back in to test that it is working correctly. Make sure that after you enter your username and password and press the login button, the website prompts you to "Please specify 2FA Code" beneath the password field. This code is the frequently changing six-digit number in the authentication application you installed earlier.

Step 3: Optional Security Enhancements

If you plan to use the Coinsqaure mobile application, you should consider a pin, fingerprint scan, or facial recognition to access the phone/application. These additional measures will provide better security in the event you lose your phone or leave it unattended.

Consider also securing your email account via 2 Factor Authentication. If a hacker ever got ahold of your email password, 2 Factor is an extra measure to prevent them from accessing your email account. Without 2 Factor preventing them from getting in, anyone with access to your email account could submit support requests to change information at Coinsqaure, ultimately granting them access to your exchange account.

Step 5: Quick Trade

After Coinsquare deposits your funds into your account, you will be able to see your current holdings from your Dashboard. This number will be the amount you sent to the exchange minus any fees associated with the funding method you chose. On the left of the Dashboard page is the quick trade option. You can use the "Trade" field to select your Canadian dollars, enter a dollar amount or a percentage that you would like to use and then select "For" and choose "XRP". Use the "GET A QUOTE" button to confirm the amount you wish to purchase and the price per unit. When you ready to move forward, use the "MAKE THIS TRADE" button. Congrats, you now have some XRP!
